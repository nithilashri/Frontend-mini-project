Manuscript preparation


  1. Templates:

    Authors are advised to use Microsoft Word or LaTeX to create your document. It is strongly recommended to use the IEEE templates that incorporate the correct formatting specifications. It is highly recommended to use the templates otherwise you might encounter problems when submitting the camera ready, where strict formatting according to the Definition Table and Glossary will be enforced.

  2. Page limit:

    Maximum number of pages allowed is 6, with 1 additional page allowed with the purchase of additional page charges during registration and final paper submission.

  3. Page Numbers, headers, and footers:

    Authors are advised not use headers, footers, or page numbers in the manuscript.s

  4. Margins:

    Papers cannot be accepted with margins smaller than those given in the table below:
    5. Margin US letter A4
    Top 0.75 inch 19mm
    Bottom 1 inch 43 mm
    Side 0.625 inch 10mm

  6. Graphics and Images:

    1. Choose vector graphics (e.g., .eps or .pdf) over bitmap (.bmp, .png, or .jpg) images because they are electronically efficient and will not conflict with conversions to PDF.

    2. All images should be checked by printing to hardcopy if the conference will be producing a book, especially when color images in your paper will be translated to black and white in print.

    3. Resolution should be between 150 and 300 dpi or less. Resolutions greater than 300 will cause down sampling; images below 300 will not render well in print.

    4. Labels within graphics should have a type point size of 4 or higher.

    5. Smooth Graphics should not be used as it often produces extremely large files with large loading time. The Smooth Graphics option is normally found in the Page Setup Dialog box.
